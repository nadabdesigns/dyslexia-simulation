For me, dyslexia feels like a challenge rooted in probability — some things click, others don’t. One of my personal struggles is with phonological awareness — the ability to recognize and distinguish between phonemes, the smallest units of sound in language. Because of this, I might read a word like nap as nip or nab — it’s subtle, but it changes everything.
